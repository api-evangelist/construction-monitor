<p>What does this update to a 1931 enactment mean for us? Construction wage work <strong>principles</strong>originally outlined are now<strong> rules</strong> “and expected to face legal challenges,” according to Fredrikson’s <em><a href="https://www.fredlaw.com/the-construction-counselor/davis-bacon-act-rule-changes-go-into-effect-on-october-23-2023-is-your-business-ready-part-1-how-the-new-dba-rule-reaches-new-workers-worksites-and-projects">The Construction Counselor</a></em>.</p>



<p>The bottom line: Use caution when considering federal infrastructure projects. Legal advisors recommend creating an internal checklist to determine DBA coverage at the pre-bid stage of a project.</p>



<p>Other DBA takeaways include:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Companies using special-purpose entities for construction projects (LLCs, JVs, partnerships) need to reassess compliance risk.</li>



<li>Contractors should re-evaluate subcontractor compliance monitoring programs and contract indemnification terms. Higher-tier contractors can now be held responsible for lower-tier subcontractor noncompliance.</li>



<li>DBA obligations will now be read into all contracts, even if they were omitted by the government originally.</li>



<li>Demolition/site remediation can be covered by DBA if the future project will/could be federally funded.</li>



<li>Department of Labor (DOL) has new authority to demand records related to DBA without initiating an investigation or other compliance action.</li>



<li>Energy infrastructure projects, such as wind, electric car chargers, and broadband, are subject to DBA if they have federal funding.</li>



<li>General contractors are now also “prime contractors” that will be held liable for noncompliance.</li>



<li>If you have a DBA exemption, check now to ensure it still applies.</li>



<li>Material suppliers had a DBA exemption, but the scope is much more limited than before.</li>



<li>Modular construction companies need to reassess if/how DBA could apply to them.</li>



<li>Recordkeeping obligations are significantly increased, along with the duty to keep those records until three years after the prime contract is closed out.</li>
</ul>



<p>Fredrikson concludes that construction wage work will cost more, especially in rural areas. “DBA wages and benefits are more likely to mirror union rates going forward.” <em><a href="https://www.constructionbusinessowner.com/strategy/prevailing-wage-work-coming">Construction Business Owner</a></em> reminds contractors prevailing construction wage work varies by location and employers can see payroll tax savings when providing bona fide benefits. (These are insurance/retirement/vacation benefits separate from the base hourly rate.)</p>



<p class="has-text-align-center">October 23, 2023<em>: The U.S. Department of Labor…announced implementation of a final rule that modernizes Davis-Bacon Act (DBA) and <a href="https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2023/08/23/2023-17221/updating-the-davis-bacon-and-related-acts-regulations">Davis-Bacon and Related Acts</a> regulations to reflect the needs of construction workers on federally funded projects better (and) …to meet the demands of the modern economy.</em></p>
